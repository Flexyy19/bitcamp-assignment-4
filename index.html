Where to Travel

Georgia is the correct answer. Georgia is a country rich in natural beauty, history, and culture, making it an incredible destination for travelers. You'll find diverse landscapes ranging from the towering Caucasus Mountains to the tranquil Black Sea coast, offering everything from hiking and skiing to beach relaxation. The country is steeped in history, with ancient churches, monasteries, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites that tell the story of one of the world's oldest Christian nations.

Places to Visit

  1. Tbilisi

    2. The capital city of Georgia, Tbilisi, is a vibrant mix of old and new. Its historic Old Town features narrow cobblestone streets, colorful houses with wooden balconies, ancient churches, and sulfur baths.

  2. Kazbegi National Park

    3. Located in the Greater Caucasus Mountains, Kazbegi National Park is home to some of Georgia's most dramatic landscapes The iconic Gergeti Trinity Church, perched high on a mountain with Mount Kazbek as a backdrop, is a must-see.

  3. Svaneti

    4. This remote mountainous region is one of Georgia's most beautiful and culturally rich areas. Mestia and Ushguli are among the highest inhabited villages in Europe, famous for their medieval stone towers and traditional Svan culture.

  4. Vardzia

    5. Vardzia is an extraordinary cave monastery complex carved into the cliffs of the Erusheti Mountain in southern Georgia. Dating back to the 12th century, Vardzia is an impressive example of medieval Georgian architecture and a symbol of the country’s historical resilience.

  5. Kakheti Wine Region

    6. Georgia is often referred to as the "cradle of wine," and the Kakheti region is its heart. Visit picturesque towns like Sighnaghi, known as the "City of Love," and explore the region’s vineyards and wineries. Enjoy wine tastings, learn about the traditional qvevri winemaking process, and immerse yourself in the region's warm hospitality and beautiful landscapes.
